CreatingWendyScript

How to build your own interpreter in C
 

Who am I?

Hello, I'm Felix Guo, a first year CS/BBA student at the University of Waterloo. I decided to create my own scripting language upon being inspired by Robert Nystrom's book, Crafting Interpreters. In his book, he details how to create an interpreter in Java. I've created my own implementation of a lot of his methods in C instead. This online blog/book is simply to document my progress and give anyone else who wants to try to create their own language/interpreter a starting point.

This series is meant to be a quick and easy way to get started. I won't go too far in depth into the world of compilers and interpreters. I won't be covering abstract syntax trees, but rather will use a series of conditionals. This definitely limits the syntactic grammar that the language can have, but will simplify the developing process a little lot.

Why C?

When I first embarked on this journey, I created the interpreter in C++ instead of C, employing various object-oriented methods, as well as various dynamic memory storage such as vectors to create WendyScript. However, my implementation wasn't very efficient or well written, so I decided to rewrite it in C, which turned out to be faster and more efficient. Of course, you could even take it one step further and write it in Assembly, but good luck doing that before I finish my undergraduate studies.

How much C do I need to know?

I'm in no way proficient in C, but you should have a strong working knowledge of memory, memory operations, pointers, modularization using header/source files, and memory operations (memset, memcpy, etc) in C.
